# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to making participation in the CyberForge community a welcoming, respectful, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of experience level, identity, or background.

By participating in this project, you agree to help create a positive, collaborative environment where everyone can learn, contribute, and grow.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive community include:

* Being respectful and considerate.
* Providing constructive feedback.
* Welcoming newcomers and helping others learn.
* Respecting differing opinions and experiences.
* Focusing on collaboration and problem-solving.
* Giving proper credit when using or improving someone else's work.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks.
* Trolling, insulting, or inflammatory comments.
* Publishing another person's private information without permission.
* Spamming or intentionally disrupting discussions.
* Promoting illegal or unethical activity.
* Any behavior that makes others feel unsafe or unwelcome.

---

## Project Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:

* GitHub Issues
* Pull Requests
* Discussions
* Documentation
* Community chats
* Any other official CyberForge communication channels

---

## Reporting Issues

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please open a private issue or contact the project maintainers.

All reports will be reviewed fairly and handled with respect for everyone's privacy.

---

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers may edit, remove, or reject comments, commits, issues, pull requests, or other contributions that violate these guidelines. Repeated or severe violations may result in temporary or permanent removal from the community.

---

## Our Goal

CyberForge exists to help people learn cybersecurity, build useful tools, and collaborate in an open and respectful environment.

Every contribution, whether it's code, documentation, bug reports, ideas, or feedback, helps make the project better.

Thank you for helping make CyberForge a welcoming community for everyone.
